# Pergamum - The City of Books and Knowledge

## History

Pergamum, situated on a dominant hilltop in Asia Minor, was one of the most important cultural metropolises of the ancient world. From the capital of the Pergamon kingdom in the Hellenistic period, it evolved into a prosperous Roman province.

Pergamum's Achievements:

- **The Library of Pergamum** - a rival of renown with the Library of Alexandria
- **The Asclepieion** - a hospital and world-renowned medical center dedicated to the god Asclepius
- **Trajan's Triumphal Arch** - an extraordinary monument to Roman power
- **The Invention of Parchment** - an alternative to Egyptian papyrus that revolutionized the preservation of knowledge

## Spiritual Message

Revelation sends Pergamum a subtle warning:

> *"I know where you live—where Satan's throne is. Yet you remain true to my name..."*

Pergamum is described as the place where Satan has his throne—not because of obvious evil, but because of eating food sacrificed to idols. The message is about subtle compromises and the danger of adapting too much to pagan culture.

## Why Visit Pergamum

1. **Ruins of the Library** - Marks of the greatest center of knowledge in the world
2. **The Asclepieion** - Ruins of the hospital that treated emperors and common people alike
3. **Pergamum's Theater** - The amphitheater where ancient dramas and comedies were performed
4. **Panoramic Views from the Top** - Extraordinary views of the Asia Minor region

## Central Themes

Pergamum reminds us:

- **Knowledge is power** - but also its danger
- **Subtle compromises** - how our values can be eroded by small concessions
- **Culture vs. Faith** - the tension between being intelligent and being faithful
- **Legacy** - how ideas and discoveries pass through time

Pergamum remains a lesson about the balance between culture and principle.
